Finishing Your Prescription


Michael Okyere | August 17th, 2025

Have you ever dealt with a bacterial illness, and soon you feel better after taking the right antibiotics for some time? Well while I have not personally dealt with a bacterial illness, the feeling of “feeling better” is great. However, when you look at the meds you have left, it is more than you anticipated. What’s the next step? Well, whatever your next decision is, do not stop taking your antibiotics.

Some may wonder why this may be the case. Well, even though you may not feel or see anymore symptoms, that does not mean the infection is completely gone. If you stop too soon, you are allowing some bacteria to survive and continue to multiply. If the bacteria survives, it becomes way stronger than it needs to be. The stronger the bacteria is, the more resistant it can be to certain antibiotics.

In fact, resistant bacteria can cause your infection to come back. According to previous data studies, bacteria can and usually doubles every 20 minutes. This means that hypothetically, across 24 hours, it could be 272, or 4.72 x 1021, times the size it was before. While this calculation does not account for the human body’s immune system (and other facts), it is still important to understand that bacteria can grow rapidly. If the infection is harder to treat next time, there will be fewer antibiotics options.

Therefore, make sure you always finish your prescription, unless your doctor says otherwise. In fact, do not be afraid to ask your doctor/pharmacist about anything unclear with your prescription. It’s better to know what you’re consuming and how it could help/harm you. And finally, make sure you protect yourself and others from antibiotic resistance.
