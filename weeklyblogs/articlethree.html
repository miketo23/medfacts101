Decoding a Prescription Label


Michael Okyere | August 24th, 2025

At some point in your life, you may have seen one of those orange prescription bottles, whether it’s laying on the street, sitting on a counter in your home, or inside a pharmacy store. Regardless of where you have seen it, most (if not all) share one thing: a prescription label. A prescription label consists of several details regarding you, the pharmacy that prescribed it to you, and the medication itself. These details include the name of the medication, the patient’s name and date of both, the patient’s home address, the patient’s phone number, directions on medication use, the RX number, the quantity, the refill frequency, the prescriber, the fill and expiration date, and the pharmacy contact information. Below is a breakdown of each factor of a prescription label.

  • Name of Medication
    • Helps patients know what drug they’re taking
  • Patient's Name and Date of Birth
    • Identification purposes
  • Home Address of Patient
    • Can help prescribers and pharmacists know where to mail drugs (if necessary)
  • Patient's Phone Number
    • Calling for emergencies
  • Direction
    • Helps the patient know how much to take, and how frequent
  • RX Number
    • Identification Number to help the pharmacy track refills (if necessary)
  • Quantity
    • How many tablets/capsules are in the bottle
  • Refill Frequency
    • Allows patient to know whether or not they need a refill of the medication
  • Prescriber
    • Giver of the medication
  • Fill and Expiration Date
    • Dates to know when the bottle was filled and when the medication expires
  • Pharmacy Contact Information
    • Helps patients know or contact the pharmacy about questions or updates on refills/prescription orders
